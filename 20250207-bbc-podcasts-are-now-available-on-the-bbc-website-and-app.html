BBC podcasts and audio are now available on BBC.com

21 July 2025

Enjoy more than 1,000 current BBC podcasts and a rich archive of shows whenever you want on BBC.com and on the BBC app.

Update on access to BBC Sounds outside the UK

Earlier this year, we launched a new audio service outside the UK on BBC.com and the BBC app. This includes access to BBC Radio 4 and BBC World Service English, thousands of hours of podcasts (including Global News Podcast, World of Secrets and Infinite Monkey Cage) as well as some of the best of the BBC's journalism and storytelling including news and history programming.

As part of the announcement, we said we planned to close BBC Sounds to audiences living outside the UK later this year, making it available exclusively to people in the UK. Anyone who lives in the UK will still be able to use the BBC Sounds app when they go on holiday abroad. We can now confirm that BBC Sounds closed for listeners based outside the UK on 21 July 2025.

For listeners outside the UK interested in ongoing access to the BBC radio stations following the closure, we've added a directory below that will allow for live listening to all of our BBC radio stations. This includes the BBC's music stations BBC Radio 1, Radio 2, and Radio 3, 6Music, 1Xtra and Asian Network, Radio 4Xtra and 5Live, all the BBC's stations from the UK nations and every local radio station in England. BBC Radio 4 and BBC World Service English remain available on BBC.com and the BBC app.

To install the BBC app on your iPhone, iPad or iPod Touch, head to the Apple App Store.

To install the BBC app on your Android phone or tablet, head to the Google Play Store.

What content is available on BBC.com and the BBC app?

Over 1,000 current and archived BBC podcasts, including hits like Global News Podcast, You're Dead to Me and Infinite Monkey Cage, plus BBC Radio 4 and World Service stations.

How long are programmes available for?

Most of the BBC's programmes are available to listen to on demand. If you're listening to a series, check that all the episodes are currently available so that you don't miss out on any parts of the story.

Episode availability is clearly displayed both on the BBC website and on the BBC app.

BBC.com website: Each episode's current availability will be displayed underneath the playback screen, just above each episode description.

BBC app: Tap on the episode and scroll down to see how long that episode is still available for.

Why are some radio programmes and podcasts not available?

Due to rights limitations, not all BBC content can be made available to international users on BBC.com and the BBC app. We endeavour to provide a comprehensive listening experience to our audience with thousands of podcasts available. We will continually be adding more content.

Will BBC radio stations still be available outside the UK?

Following the closure of the BBC Sounds product outside the UK on 21 July 2025, we have made alternative arrangements for listeners to continue accessing BBC radio stations internationally.

Whilst the BBC Radio 4 and BBC World Service English live streams remain available via BBC.com and the BBC app, other BBC stations such as our music stations (Radio 1, Radio 2, Radio 3, 6 Music, 1Xtra, Asian Network), Radio 4Xtra and 5Live as well as all the BBC's stations from the UK nations and every local radio station in England can still be streamed live via the links below.

Will I be able to access Welsh language and Gaelic podcasts following the closure of BBC Sounds outside the UK?

Yes, a range of Welsh and Gaelic podcasts will continue to be available internationally via bbc.com/audio and the BBC app.

Closure of BBC Sounds

When will BBC Sounds become unavailable outside the UK?

We can confirm that BBC Sounds closed outside the UK on Monday, 21 July 2025.

Why did you make this change?

BBC Studios is a commercial subsidiary of the BBC and is focused on bringing our trusted, world class journalism and storytelling to international audiences. Earlier this year, BBC Studios launched a new audio section on bbc.com/the BBC app (a commercial service) outside the UK.

Revenue generated by these services is returned to the BBC to support its public purpose in the UK.

As part of this announcement, we said we would close BBC Sounds outside the UK and make it exclusively available to listeners in the UK.

For how long will UK residents be able to use BBC Sounds whilst abroad?

UK listeners who go on holiday will still be able to use the BBC Sounds app abroad for around a month

How to use audio in BBC.com and BBC app

How can I search for specific audio content?

You can search for specific audio content using keywords, titles or contributors in the search bar at the top of the BBC app or BBC.com website.

Are there categories to help me find content?

Yes, you can explore content through categories such as News and History.

Can I download audio content for offline listening?

Yes, downloading audio podcasts is now supported in the latest version of the BBC mobile app.

To download an episode, simply search for the podcast or browse via the homepage, categories, or schedules. On the podcast episode page, tap the 'Download' option near the top of the screen.

To access your downloaded content:

  1. Go to More in the app menu (More is accessed via the apps footer menu, visible at the bottom of your screen on Index pages.)
  2. Tap Saved Items
  3. Navigate to the Downloads section

From there, you can play your downloaded podcasts by tapping the play icon, or remove them by swiping the individual podcast to the left.

Will I need to be signed into BBC account to listen to Audio?

No, you don't need to be signed in to listen to audio. However, a BBC account will be required to follow, save, or download content.

VisitBBC account helpif you need help or support with signing up for a BBC account.

  • Forgotten your password? SeeHow do I change my password?for information on what to do.
  • Need to change the email address associated with your account? Visit theHow do I update my email address?page on the BBC account website.

Why have I lost all my subscribed and bookmarked content from BBC Sounds?

Unfortunately, your subscribed and bookmarked content is not transferred automatically. You'll need to manually set up your subscriptions and bookmarks again.Please log into your BBC account and add it to your new "Follow" and "Saved" lists.

I'm in the UK. Why am I presented with BBC Audio instead of BBC Sounds?

It is likely you are using a VPN (Virtual Private Network) and are accessingBBC.com, which is intended for audiences outside the UK. If you disable your VPN, you should be redirected back tobbc.co.uk, intended for UK users.

You may find something that doesn't work within Audio for BBC.com or BBC app as you expected it to. If that's the case, please let us know bysending a message. We read every piece of feedback and give it serious consideration as we develop our plans to improve the BBC.com site and the BBC app.
